About the Program

The Department of Mathematical Sciences is organizing a Bridge Course in Analysis, Algebra, and Linear Algebra under the PM-USHA Scheme. This course is designed for incoming first-year M.Sc. Mathematics students, aiming to bridge the academic gap between undergraduate and postgraduate studies.

The program focuses on strengthening students' understanding of key concepts and enhancing analytical and problem-solving skills. It is particularly beneficial for students from socially and economically disadvantaged groups (SEDG). Sessions will be interactive, combining lectures and problem-solving activities led by experienced and reputed teachers.

  • Focus on core foundations in Analysis, Algebra, and Linear Algebra
  • Emphasis on conceptual clarity and problem-solving
  • Aims to bridge the gap between undergraduate and postgraduate coursework
  • Combined sessions of lectures and problem solving

Syllabus

Real Analysis

  • Completeness Property of R: bounds, supremum, infimum
  • Properties: Archimedean, density, uncountability
  • Sequences and Series: convergence, MCT, BWT, Cauchy sequences
  • Series Tests: convergence tests, special series
  • Limits and Continuity: types, differentiability

Abstract Algebra

  • Groups, subgroups, cosets, cyclic and abelian groups
  • Group examples: $S_{n}$, $A_{n}$, $D_{n}$, $Q_{8}$
  • Homomorphism, Isomorphism
  • Fields and Rings

Linear Algebra

  • Matrices and systems, diagonalization
  • Vector spaces, fields, subspaces
  • Span, linear independence, basis, dimension
